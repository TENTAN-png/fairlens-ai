Build all FairLens AI interfaces using simple, human-friendly language. Avoid displaying technical AI fairness terms such as "Disparate Impact", "Statistical Parity Difference", "Equalized Odds", "Counterfactual Fairness", "SHAP", "LIME", "Bias Mitigation Algorithms", or other research terminology directly to users.

The platform should be designed for HR managers, recruiters, compliance officers, business leaders, educators, government officials, and non-technical users.

Replace technical terms with clear, easy-to-understand labels.

Examples:

Instead of "Disparate Impact"
Use:

* Fairness Check
* Group Fairness Score
* Equal Opportunity Score

Instead of "Statistical Parity Difference"
Use:

* Outcome Difference
* Fairness Gap
* Decision Balance

Instead of "Counterfactual Fairness"
Use:

* Decision Consistency Check
* Identity Swap Test
* Fair Decision Verification

Instead of "Bias Detection"
Use:

* Fairness Review
* Equity Check
* Decision Audit

Instead of "Mitigation"
Use:

* Improvement Suggestions
* Fairness Improvements
* Recommended Fixes

Instead of "Protected Attributes"
Use:

* Sensitive Information
* Personal Characteristics
* Diversity Factors

Instead of "SHAP Feature Importance"
Use:

* Main Influencing Factors
* Key Decision Drivers
* Top Factors Affecting Results

Instead of "Root Cause Analysis"
Use:

* Why This Happened
* Main Reasons Behind the Results
* What Is Driving These Decisions

Every feature should include a short explanation card written in plain English.

Examples:

Fairness Health Score
"Measures how equally opportunities are distributed across different groups."

Decision Consistency Check
"Checks whether changing a person's background information would unfairly change the outcome."

Fairness Timeline
"Shows how fairness levels have changed over time."

Dataset Health Report
"Checks data quality, missing information, balance, and possible risks before analysis."

AI Fairness Assistant
"Ask questions about your results and receive easy-to-understand explanations and recommendations."

Compliance Check
"Reviews whether your AI system follows common fairness and transparency guidelines."

Improvement Simulator
"Test possible changes and instantly see how they affect fairness and performance."

Fairness Alerts
"Receive warnings when potential unfair treatment is detected."

Bias Comparison Studio
"Compare before-and-after results to see whether fairness has improved."

Use simple language, visual indicators, color-coded risk levels, progress bars, scorecards, and business-friendly explanations throughout the platform.

The interface should feel similar to Google Analytics, Grammarly, or Google Search Console: powerful internally but extremely easy to understand for non-technical users.
